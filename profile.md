# About

Most teams are using AI. Very few are building AI-native.

There is a real difference between deploying AI tools on top of existing engineering workflows and redesigning the process itself around AI capabilities. I write and think seriously about that gap — and about what it takes, practically, to close it.

## What I Work On

My focus is **AI-native software engineering**: the discipline of building systems where language models are load-bearing components rather than optional accelerants. That means thinking carefully about:

- **Software architecture** — how LLMs and AI components appear in the core architecture diagram, not in a footnote
- **Probabilistic quality engineering** — evaluation frameworks, regression suites, and human review loops for systems whose outputs are distributions, not deterministic values
- **Retrieval-augmented systems** — grounding model outputs in retrieved, citable, domain-specific information rather than model memory
- **Continuous evaluation infrastructure** — automated quality metrics, feedback mechanisms, versioned prompt management, and alerting for output quality degradation
- **Team and organisational design** — what AI-native engineering teams look like, how they are structured, and how engineering organisations make the transition

## The Blog: Engineering Insights

This blog is where I publish the AI-Native Engineering series — ten posts covering the full landscape of what it means to build and lead in an AI-native world.

**Published:**
1. [What is AI-Native Software Engineering](https://anubhaws.sg-host.com/what-is-ai-native-software-engineering/)
2. [Why AI Will Change Software Development](https://anubhaws.sg-host.com/why-ai-will-change-software-development/)
3. [Architecture of AI-Native Applications](https://anubhaws.sg-host.com/architecture-of-ai-native-applications/)
4. [Prompt Engineering & Context Engineering](https://anubhaws.sg-host.com/prompt-engineering-context-engineering-mastering-llm-performance-through-dual-optimization-strategies/)

**Coming next:** AI coding assistants, feedback loops for reliable AI systems, AI agents in development workflows, designing AI-native engineering teams, and building the AI-native engineering organisation.

The writing is practical and evidence-based. Every claim about developer productivity, adoption rates, or system design patterns is cited from controlled research or real deployment data — not industry speculation.

## How I Think About This

The difficulty of the AI-native transition is not technical. Most teams already have access to the APIs, the frameworks, and the compute.

The difficulty is organisational and epistemic. Engineering teams optimised for deterministic systems struggle with probabilistic quality norms. Product teams accustomed to feature specifications struggle with capability-based roadmaps. QA processes built for regression testing break down when "regression" means a measurable change in output distribution.

AI-native engineering requires new mental models, new processes, and new team structures — not just new tools. That is what this blog is about.
